Trigger Finger
2026
Ran Google MediaPipe on my laptop to watch for the finger gun flick motion, then streamed the trigger signal over to a Raspberry Pi driving a servo. Every time I flick my finger like I'm firing, the servo snaps in sync in the real world. Dumb little toy, but there is something weirdly satisfying about a gesture I make in the air physically moving something on my desk.
MediaPipe Raspberry Pi Gesture Trigger
Motion Reflex
2026
Built a proper power rail on a breadboard so the Raspberry Pi could cleanly feed both a PCA9685 servo driver and a PIR motion sensor without browning anything out. The Pi reads the PIR, and the moment it picks up a hand passing nearby, it fires the servo and then sits on a 4 second cooldown before it can trigger again. First time wiring something that reacts to the room instead of a keyboard, and the breadboard finally clicked as a real thing instead of a diagram in a tutorial.
PIR Sensor Breadboard Raspberry Pi
Telepathic Servos
2026
Wired up two MG90S servos to a Raspberry Pi 4 through a PCA9685 driver, then tied them to a hand tracker running on my laptop webcam. The camera reads my finger positions and moves the servos in real time, kind of like giving the computer a telepathic link to my hand. First real embedded build, and seeing software reach into the physical world felt completely different from shipping another web app.
Raspberry Pi Hand Tracking First Embedded Build

Why I killed Kairo
Apr 2026
A post-mortem on shutting down Kairo, the four questions I now run every new idea through, and why being a GPT wrapper isn't a business.
Read

So I killed Kairo. For context, Kairo was this AI thing I built to help high school students organize their extracurriculars. Clubs, competitions, deadlines, all in one place. Sounds useful, right? That's what I thought too. Then I actually watched the numbers come in. Like 600 visits, 70% bounce rate, 80 signups. And even the people who signed up, they'd only open it maybe once or twice a month. Because realistically, how often do you actually update your extracurriculars? Not daily, not weekly. Once a month, max. That's not a product. That's a tool you forget about between uses.

But honestly, the numbers weren't even the main reason. The real thing was when I sat down and asked myself this. What can Kairo do that Claude can't already do? And the answer was basically nothing. I was literally just building a GPT wrapper. A UI on top of something Anthropic or OpenAI is gonna ship for free in six months. That's not sustainable, that's not innovative, and I just don't wanna build that.

So I shut it down. Now before I start anything new I run four questions through my head. Will people come back? Does it get better as AI gets better? Can Claude already do this? And is this a real problem or just fun to ship? Misses any of them, I don't touch it.
